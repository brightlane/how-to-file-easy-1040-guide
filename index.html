Step-by-step IRS-style instructions for new taxpayers

📋 Filing Status & Exemptions

Filing Status Options:
  • Single
  • Married Filing Jointly
  • Head of Household
  • Married Filing Separately
Example: Select **Single** if you are unmarried.
💡 Tip: Your filing status will affect your standard deduction and tax rates.

💼 Line 7: Wages, Salaries, Tips

Example: Wages from Employer A = $40,000
Report all wages as shown on your W-2.
💡 Tip: Check your W-2 for the correct amount in Box 1.

💵 Line 8: Interest Income

Example: Bank A Interest = $100, Bank B Interest = $50
Total Interest = $150
💡 Tip: Don't forget to report **tax-exempt** interest if applicable.

📈 Line 9: Dividends

Example: Ordinary dividends = $200, Qualified dividends = $100
Total dividends = $300
💡 Tip: **Qualified dividends** are taxed at a lower rate.

🛣️ Line 10: IRA, Pension, and Annuities

Example: Early IRA withdrawal: $2,500
💡 Tip: If you are under 59½, you may owe an additional 10% penalty on early IRA withdrawals.

🔢 Line 11: Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

Example: Total income = $40,000 + $150 + $300 = $40,450
💡 Tip: Subtract deductions like student loan interest or IRA contributions here to find your AGI.

📉 Deductions (Line 12 - 15)

Example: Standard Deduction for **Single** = $12,550
Subtract this from your AGI to find **Taxable Income**.
💡 Tip: You can choose between the **Standard Deduction** or **Itemizing** your deductions.

📊 Tax Liability & Credits

Example: Tax liability is calculated using IRS tax tables based on **Taxable Income**.
Subtract any credits like the **Child Tax Credit**.
💡 Tip: Tax credits directly reduce your **Tax Liability**.

💸 Refund or Amount Owed (Line 33 - 36)

Example: Total tax liability = $2,500, Payments made = $3,000
Refund = $500
💡 Tip: If you've overpaid, you'll receive a refund. If underpaid, you will owe the IRS.

📝 Schedule 1: Additional Income

Example: Include self-employment income or other sources of income not reported elsewhere.

⚙️ Adjustments to Income

Example: Deduct student loan interest or IRA contributions here.

💰 Schedule 2: Additional Taxes

Include taxes like self-employment tax, alternative minimum tax (AMT), and others.

🏅 Schedule 3: Tax Credits

This includes credits like the **Education Credit**, **Child and Dependent Care Credit**, etc.

Educational purposes only. Always verify with IRS for final numbers.
