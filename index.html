✅ PART 2 — STEP-BY-STEP FORM 1040 GUIDE

🦉 Step 1 — Gather Your Forms

💡 Professor Hootington: “Before touching the 1040, make sure you have everything ready.”

  • W‑2 from your employer(s)
  • 1099s (freelance, interest, dividends)
  • Last year’s tax return (if available)
  • Receipts for deductions (education, charity, medical)
Extra Tips: Being organized makes filling the 1040 much faster and reduces mistakes.

🦉 Step 2 — Fill in Your Personal Info (Lines 1–7)

💡 Professor Hootington: “Every number counts, especially your Social Security number!”

  • Line 1 – Your name and Social Security number
  • Line 2 – Spouse info if married
  • Line 3–7 – Dependents, standard info

🦉 Step 3 — Add Up Your Income (Lines 8–15)

💡 Professor Hootington: “Add everything carefully, double-check the math.”

  • Line 8 – Wages, salaries, tips from W-2
  • Line 9 – Taxable interest
  • Line 10 – Dividends
  • Line 11 – IRA distributions
  • Line 12 – Pensions and annuities
  • Line 13 – Social Security benefits (taxable portion)
  • Line 14 – Other income (prizes, gambling winnings)
  • Line 15 – Total Income
Extra Tips: Copy numbers directly from your forms. Don’t estimate.

🦉 Step 4 — Adjustments & AGI (Lines 16–20)

💡 Professor Hootington: “Adjustments reduce your taxable income—don’t skip them!”

  • Line 16 – Student loan interest, IRA contributions
  • Line 17 – Self-employment expenses
  • Line 18 – Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

🦉 Step 5 — Deductions & Taxable Income (Lines 19–22)

💡 Professor Hootington: “Pick the bigger deduction: standard vs. itemized.”

  • Line 19 – Standard deduction or itemized deductions
  • Line 20 – Taxable income (AGI minus deductions)

🦉 Step 6 — Tax, Credits, Payments (Lines 23–30)

💡 Professor Hootington: “Apply credits—they can reduce your tax or even give you a refund!”

  • Line 23 – Tax owed based on taxable income
  • Line 24 – Credits (child tax, education, retirement savings)
  • Line 25 – Other taxes (self-employment, AMT)
  • Line 26 – Total tax
  • Line 27 – Federal tax withheld (from W-2/1099)
  • Line 28 – Estimated payments
  • Line 29 – Total payments
  • Line 30 – Refund or amount owed
Extra Tips: Credits reduce your tax dollar-for-dollar. Always check eligibility!

🦉 Step 7 — Sign & Submit

💡 Professor Hootington: “No signature = no submission! Always sign and date your form.”

2. What if I make a mistake?

Small mistakes can be corrected. Professor Hootington says: “Check twice before submitting, and if needed, file an amended return.”

3. How do I know if I get a refund?

Your total payments minus total tax determines refund or amount owed. Professor Hootington reminds: “Use your calculator and double-check!”

4. What forms do I need?

  • 1040 Form
  • Schedules A, B, C, D, SE (if applicable)
  • W-2 and 1099 forms
