1️⃣ Line 1 – Wages, Salaries, Tips

Enter total wages from all W-2 forms. Example: Employer A: $30,000 Employer B: $10,000 Total: $40,000
💡 Tip: Ensure your SSN and name match your Social Security card to avoid IRS errors.

2️⃣ Line 2 – Taxable Interest

Include taxable interest from banks or investment accounts. Bank A: $50 Bank B: $150 Total: $200
💡 Tip: Exclude tax-free municipal bonds.

3️⃣ Line 3 – Dividends

Ordinary and qualified dividends: Ordinary: $100 Qualified: $50 Total: $150
💡 Tip: Qualified dividends are taxed at lower capital gains rates.

4️⃣ Line 4 – IRA Distributions

Enter IRA distributions. Example: Traditional IRA: $5,000 Roth IRA distributions (tax-free): $0
💡 Tip: Only taxable amounts count. Roth withdrawals may be tax-free.

5️⃣ Line 5 – Pensions and Annuities

Enter taxable pensions/annuities. Example: Pension: $12,000 Annuity: $1,000 Total taxable: $13,000
💡 Tip: Check 1099-R forms carefully for taxable amounts.

➕ Additional Income

Covers self-employment, rental, unemployment, prizes, and more. Include all IRS-defined sources of income.

⚖️ Adjustments to Income

Student loan interest, IRA contributions, educator expenses, and other deductions reduce AGI.

💰 Self-Employment & AMT

Includes self-employment tax, alternative minimum tax, and other additional taxes required by the IRS.

🎯 Education, Childcare, and Other Credits

Apply applicable credits to reduce overall tax owed, following IRS rules and limits.

📂 Itemized Deductions

Includes medical expenses, state/local taxes, mortgage interest, charitable contributions. Compare to standard deduction.

💵 Reporting Interest & Dividends

All interest and dividend income reported; attach 1099-INT and 1099-DIV as needed.

📊 Capital Gains & Losses

Report all sales of stocks, bonds, and other assets; calculate net gain/loss per IRS instructions.
