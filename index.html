1. Filing Status: How to Determine Your Filing Status

Your filing status affects your tax rates and eligibility for deductions and credits. Select the right status for your situation.

1.1. Single vs. Married Filing Jointly

Learn how your filing status impacts your tax deductions and credits.

2. Enter Personal Information: SSN, Address, and Dependents

Provide your full name, Social Security Number (SSN), address, and information about your dependents.

2.1. Reporting Dependents

List any children or relatives you financially support. For each dependent, include their name, SSN, and relationship to you.

3. Income Section: How to Report All Sources of Income

Report all income sources such as wages, interest, dividends, and self-employment income.

3.1. Reporting Wages from Form W-2

If you are employed, your employer will send you **Form W-2**, which lists your wages and taxes withheld. Use this to report your income on the 1040 form.

3.2. Reporting Interest and Dividends

If you have investment income, report it from **Form 1099-INT** or **Form 1099-DIV**.

4. Adjustments to Income: Deductions You Can Claim

Some expenses can reduce your taxable income, such as student loan interest, IRA contributions, or tuition fees.

4.1. Student Loan Interest Deduction

You may deduct up to $2,500 of student loan interest. Check **Form 1098-E** for the amount of interest you paid.

5. Taxable Income: How to Calculate Your Taxable Income

Your taxable income is the amount after subtracting deductions from your gross income.

5.1. Standard Deduction vs. Itemizing

Decide whether to take the **standard deduction** or to itemize deductions. The standard deduction for **single filers** is $12,550 (2021). Itemizing may benefit you if you have large deductible expenses.

6. Tax Calculation: How to Calculate Your Total Tax Liability

Use the **IRS tax tables** to calculate your tax based on your taxable income.

7. Tax Credits: Maximize Your Refunds

Claim any tax credits you're eligible for, such as the **Child Tax Credit** or the **Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)**.

7.1. Child Tax Credit

For qualifying children under 17, you can claim up to $2,000 per child. You may qualify even if you owe no taxes.

8. Other Taxes: Self-Employment Tax and More

Some filers need to pay additional taxes, like **self-employment tax** (from Schedule SE), additional Medicare tax, or unreported Social Security and Medicare taxes.

8.1. Self-Employment Tax (Schedule SE)

If you're self-employed, use **Schedule SE** to calculate and report your self-employment tax. This tax helps fund your Social Security and Medicare benefits.

9. Payments & Refunds: How to Calculate What You Owe or Get Back

Enter the taxes already paid via withholding or estimated payments. Compare this to your total tax liability to determine if you owe additional taxes or are entitled to a refund.

9.1. Federal Income Tax Withholding

Check **Form W-2** to see how much federal income tax has been withheld by your employer. This amount is subtracted from your calculated tax liability.

10. Signature & Submission: Final Steps for Filing

Sign and date your return. You must also include any necessary payments if you owe taxes. Once your return is complete, submit it to the IRS either via mail or electronically using the IRS e-file system.

Form 1040: Full Breakdown of Line Numbers

Form 1040 - Page 1

Below is a breakdown of the first page of IRS Form 1040. These are the lines you’ll fill out to report your basic income and deductions:

Line 1: Enter your **total income** here (from W-2, 1099 forms, etc.)
Line 2: Adjustments to income, such as student loan interest or IRA contributions.
Line 3: Your **adjusted gross income (AGI)**, after adjustments.
Line 4: Standard deduction or itemized deductions. Choose the one that gives you the higher deduction.
Line 5: Your **taxable income** after subtracting deductions.
Line 6: Use the IRS tax tables to find your **tax liability** based on taxable income.
Line 7: Enter any **tax credits** you qualify for, such as Child Tax Credit or Education Credit.
Line 8: Subtract credits from your tax liability to reduce the amount you owe.
Line 9: If your withholding or payments exceed your tax, you'll get a **refund** here.

Form 1040 - Page 2

Below are the continuation lines from **Page 2** of the 1040 Form:

Line 10: Additional taxes (self-employment, etc.).
Line 11: Total taxes owed, including additional taxes from Page 1.
Line 12: Enter any additional payments you've made (e.g., estimated tax payments).
Line 13: Determine if you owe taxes or if you’re due for a refund by comparing total payments to tax liability.
Line 14: If you owe, make your **payment** by check or using IRS electronic payment methods.
Line 15: If you’re due a refund, you can elect to have it directly deposited into your bank account here.
Line 16: Signature section where you sign your return. Don’t forget to sign!
Line 17: Enter your **phone number** and **email** for contact purposes.
Line 18: Indicate if someone else prepared your return and their details.

This section includes all necessary lines from **Page 1 and Page 2** of the IRS Form 1040. These lines guide you through the tax calculation and final steps for filing. Make sure to double-check all calculations and follow the IRS instructions on the form for any additional details or requirements.
