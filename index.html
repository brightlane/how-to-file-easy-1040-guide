📋 PART 1: The Basics

L1 Name, Address, and SSN ▼

This is where the IRS gets to know you. Ensure your name matches your Social Security card exactly.

🦉 Pro Tip: Even a small typo here can delay your refund by weeks!
L2 Filing Status ▼

Are you Single, Married, or Head of Household? This determines your tax rate and standard deduction.

💰 PART 2: Income (Money In)

L1z Wages from W-2s ▼

The amount from Box 1 of all your W-2 forms. This is the "bread and butter" of most tax returns.

Gather these:
  • All W-2 forms from employers
  • Final paystubs (for verification)
L8 Other Income (Gig Work, etc) ▼

Did you drive for Uber, sell crafts, or do freelance web development? That goes here via Schedule 1.

✂️ PART 3: Deductions (Lower Your Bill)

L12 Standard vs. Itemized Deduction ▼

Most people take the Standard Deduction. It's a flat amount the IRS lets you subtract from your income so you don't pay taxes on it.

🦉 Professor's Wisdom: Think of this as a "discount code" the IRS gives everyone!

🎁 PART 4: Credits & Payments

L19 Child Tax Credit ▼

If you have dependents under 17, you might get a significant credit here.

L25 Federal Income Tax Withheld ▼

Look at your W-2 Box 2. This is money you ALREADY paid throughout the year. We want to make sure you get any extra back!

🎉 PART 5: The Grand Finale

L34 Your Refund Amount ▼

If L33 (Payments) is bigger than L24 (Total Tax), the IRS owes YOU money! 🥳

L37 Amount You Owe ▼

If your taxes were higher than what you paid, you'll need to settle up with the IRS. Pay by April 15th to avoid penalties!
