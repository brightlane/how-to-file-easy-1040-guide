Page 1 – Income & Adjustments (Lines 1–10)

Line 1a – Wages, salaries, tips
All the money you earned from jobs. Copy Box 1 from your W-2 forms.
Tip 1: Check that all your jobs are included.
Tip 2: Add multiple W-2s together.
Tip 3: Use your pay stubs if no W-2.
Line 1b – Household employee wages not on W‑2
Money earned babysitting, mowing lawns, or other work without a W-2.
Tip 1: Keep a notebook of payments.
Tip 2: Report all cash jobs.
Tip 3: Include even small amounts.
Line 1c – Tip income not on W‑2
Tips from jobs or small businesses like lemonade stands.
Tip 1: Record tips as you get them.
Tip 2: Report tips over $20/month to your employer.
Tip 3: Include tips in income to avoid IRS issues.
Line 2a – Tax-exempt interest
Interest that is not taxed, like some government bonds.
Tip 1: Still report tax-exempt interest.
Tip 2: Look at 1099-INT form.
Tip 3: Helps show all money earned.
Line 2b – Taxable interest
Interest you earned that is taxed.
Tip 1: Check 1099-INT.
Tip 2: Include banks, CDs, bonds.
Tip 3: Add each source carefully.
Line 3a – Qualified dividends
Dividends from stocks taxed at lower rate.
Tip 1: Use 1099-DIV.
Tip 2: Only report qualified portion.
Tip 3: Check for correct “qualified” box.
Line 3b – Ordinary dividends
Dividends not qualified for low tax rate.
Tip 1: Report separately from qualified.
Tip 2: Taxed at normal rate.
Tip 3: Include reinvested dividends.
Line 4a – IRA distributions
Money withdrawn from retirement account.
Tip 1: Include early withdrawals.
Tip 2: Check 1099-R.
Tip 3: Some withdrawals may have penalties.
Line 4b – Taxable IRA distributions
Only the taxable part of your IRA withdrawal.
Tip 1: Use IRS worksheet for taxable amount.
Tip 2: Early withdrawals may add penalties.
Tip 3: Ask an adult if unsure.
Line 5a – Pensions and annuities
Money received from pensions or annuities.
Tip 1: Use Form 1099-R.
Tip 2: Some may not be taxable.
Tip 3: Check taxable vs non-taxable amounts.
Line 5b – Taxable pensions
Only the part of pensions that is taxable.
Tip 1: Copy “taxable amount” from 1099-R.
Tip 2: Only report taxable portion.
Tip 3: Check instructions carefully.
Line 6a – Social Security benefits
Money received from Social Security.
Tip 1: Check SSA-1099 form.
Tip 2: Some benefits may not be taxable.
Tip 3: Only report total received here.
Line 6b – Taxable Social Security
Only the part of Social Security that is taxable.
Tip 1: Use IRS worksheet to calculate taxable amount.
Tip 2: Combine with other income to determine tax.
Tip 3: Ask for help if numbers are confusing.
Line 7 – Other income
Side jobs, prizes, gambling winnings, or other income not counted above.
Tip 1: Keep a list of small jobs.
Tip 2: Include all unexpected income.
Tip 3: Helps calculate total income correctly.
Line 8 – Total income
Add lines 1–7 to get total income.
Tip 1: Double-check your addition.
Tip 2: This is the number IRS will use for your AGI.
Tip 3: Use a calculator to avoid mistakes.
Line 9 – Adjustments to income (Schedule 1)
Special subtractions like student loan interest or contributions to retirement accounts.
Tip 1: Fill out Schedule 1 to see what you can subtract.
Tip 2: Include tuition, student loans, self-employment deductions.
Tip 3: Subtracting helps lower tax and increase refund.
Line 10 – Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)
Total income minus adjustments from line 9.
Tip 1: AGI is the “clean income” IRS looks at.
Tip 2: Use it to calculate deductions and credits.
Tip 3: Double-check before moving to next page.
