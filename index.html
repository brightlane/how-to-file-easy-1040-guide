First-Time Filer 1040 Guide

Step-by-step IRS-style instructions for new taxpayers

1 Determine Your Filing Status

Choosing the correct filing status is critical. It determines your tax rates, deductions, and credits.

Filing Status Options:

  • Single
  • Married Filing Jointly
  • Married Filing Separately
  • Head of Household
  • Qualifying Widow(er)
Tip: Use IRS worksheets if you're unsure about your filing status.

2 Report Your Income

All sources of income must be reported on Form 1040, including wages, dividends, interest, and self-employment income.

Document Checklist:

  • W-2 forms from employers
  • 1099 forms for freelance work
  • Bank interest and dividend statements
Wages: $45,000 Interest: $120 Dividends: $200 Total Income: $45,320
Tip: Keep digital copies of all forms for easy reference and audit protection.

3 Claim Deductions and Credits

Deductions and credits reduce the amount of tax you owe. Make sure you understand which ones you qualify for.

Common Deductions:

  • Student loan interest
  • Medical expenses
  • Charitable donations

Common Credits:

  • Child Tax Credit
  • Earned Income Credit
Tip: Double-check each deduction and credit; small errors can trigger IRS questions.
