🦉 Professor Hootington’s First-Time Tax Filing Guide

Step 1 — Gather Your Papers

What you need:

  • W‑2 from your job
  • 1099 forms if you did freelance or earned interest
  • Receipts for deductions (like education or charity)
  • Last year’s tax return (if you have it)
💬 Professor Hootington says: “Think of this as collecting all your school supplies before starting a project. You can’t work without them!”

Step 2 — Fill in Your Personal Info

Form 1040:

  • Your name, address, Social Security number
  • Spouse’s info (if married)
  • Dependents (kids or others you care for)
💬 Tip: “Check every letter and number—tiny mistakes can slow things down.”

Step 3 — Add Up Your Income (Lines 1–8)

  • Line 1: Wages from W‑2s
  • Line 2: Interest from bank accounts
  • Line 3: Dividends from stocks
  • Line 4–7: Retirement money, Social Security, side jobs, prizes
  • Line 8: Total income = all above added together
💬 Professor Hootington tip: “This is like counting all your allowance, gifts, and money earned in one pile.”

Step 4 — Subtract Adjustments (Schedule 1 / Line 9–10)

Things that lower your taxable income:

  • Student loan interest
  • IRA contributions
  • Educator expenses
  • Self-employment expenses
  • HSA contributions

Line 10: Adjusted Gross Income (AGI) = Total Income – Adjustments

💬 Tip: “This is your ‘clean income,’ the number the IRS really cares about.”

Step 5 — Choose Your Deduction (Lines 11–12)

  • Standard deduction: fixed amount everyone can take
  • Itemized deduction: add up things like medical, mortgage, charity
  • Line 12: Taxable Income = AGI – Deduction
💬 Tip: “Pick the bigger deduction to pay less tax.”

Step 6 — Calculate Tax (Lines 13–15 / Schedule 2)

  • Line 13: Tax based on taxable income (look up IRS tables)
  • Schedule 2: Extra taxes (if self-employed or AMT)
  • Line 15: Total tax = Tax + Extra taxes
💬 Tip: “Double-check numbers and include extra taxes if they apply.”

Step 7 — Apply Payments & Credits (Lines 16–20 / Schedule 3)

  • Line 16: Federal tax withheld from W‑2/1099
  • Line 17: Estimated payments made
  • Schedule 3: Nonrefundable & refundable credits
  • Line 20: Total payments = all above
💬 Tip: “Credits reduce your tax, sometimes giving you a refund!”

Step 8 — Find Refund or Amount Owed (Lines 21–25)

  • Compare total tax (Line 15) vs total payments (Line 20)
  • If payments > tax → refund
  • If tax > payments → amount owed
  • Line 25: Sign and date
💬 Tip: “Sign your tax return or it’s like turning in homework unsigned!”

Step 9 — Complete Supporting Schedules (If Needed)

  • Schedule A: Itemized deductions
  • Schedule B: Interest & Dividends
  • Schedule C: Business income
  • Schedule D: Capital gains/losses
  • Schedule E: Rental/Partnership income
  • Schedule F: Farm income/loss
  • Schedule SE: Self-employment tax
  • Schedule 8812: Child tax credit
💬 Tip: “These feed numbers into your 1040. Finish them first if you need them.”

✅ Professor Hootington’s Quick Tips for First-Time Filers

  • Take it step by step 🦉
  • Use last year’s return as a guide if you have it
  • Keep receipts and forms handy
  • Double-check every number
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help

🦉 Professor Hootington’s How to File Form 1040 Line-by-Line

Page 1 — Income & Adjustments (Lines 1–10)

Line 1a – Wages, salaries, tips
All the money you earned from jobs. Copy Box 1 from your W-2 forms.
Tip 1: Double-check that you include all your W-2s.
Tip 2: Add them up if you worked more than one job.
Tip 3: Ensure your numbers match what your employer gave you.
