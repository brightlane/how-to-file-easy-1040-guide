Professor Owl Tax Guide

A simple tax guide for beginners, explained like you are in fifth grade.

Start Filing

Step 1: Get your papers

Professor Owl says: Put all your tax papers in one folder.

This helps you stay organized before you begin.

What it means: Gather your W-2s, 1099s, and receipts.

Tip 1: Find your W-2 forms.
Tip 2: Find your 1099 forms.
Tip 3: Keep receipts in one place.

Step 2: Add your income

Professor Owl says: Write down every dollar you earned.

Start with the biggest numbers first.

What it means: Write down all the money you made.

Tip 1: Look at Box 1 on your W-2.
Tip 2: Add money from side jobs if needed.
Tip 3: Use a calculator so the numbers stay exact.

Step 3: Use deductions and credits

Professor Owl says: Deductions and credits can help lower your tax bill.

Credits are usually the bigger win.

What it means: These can help lower your tax bill.

Tip 1: Deductions lower the amount that gets taxed.
Tip 2: Credits lower the tax itself.
Tip 3: A credit is usually the bigger win.

How to Read a Tax Table

Income Rate Example Tax
$0 – $10,000 10% $1,000 if you earned $10,000
$10,001 – $40,000 12% $3,600 if you earned $30,000 in this bracket
$40,001 – $85,000 22% $9,900 if you earned $45,000 in this bracket

Tip: Only the money in each bracket gets taxed at that rate.

Final check

Professor Owl says: Double-check every number before you file.

Then sign your return and click the finish button.

Tip 1: Check for missing numbers.
Tip 2: Make sure your name and Social Security number are correct.
Tip 3: Sign your return before you send it.
Finish Filing
