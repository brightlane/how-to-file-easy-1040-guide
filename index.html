🦉 How to File Your Taxes with Professor Hootington

Step 1: Gather Your Income Papers

Why do we need these? The government wants to know how much money you earned this year. Let's gather everything so we can start your taxes!

  • W-2: Your boss gives this if you have a job.
  • 1099: Shows money you made from extra work or interest from the bank.
  • Other income: Money from side jobs, tips, or rentals.
📝 Professor Tip: Keep these papers together in a folder so nothing gets lost!

Step 2: Gather Your Expense & Deduction Papers

These help lower your taxes. We call them deductions.

  • Receipts for charity donations.
  • Medical expenses if you’re listing them.
  • Education expenses (tuition, books).
📝 Professor Tip: Scan or take photos of receipts so you never lose them.

Step 3: Collect Personal Info

  • Social Security numbers for you, spouse, and dependents.
  • Bank info for direct deposit of your refund.
📝 Professor Tip: Double-check all numbers — one wrong digit can delay your refund!

Step 4: Read Your W-2 Easily

Let’s see what your W-2 tells us:

  • Box 1: All the money you earned.
  • Box 2: Tax already taken out by your boss.
  • Box 3: Social Security wages.
📌 Professor Tip: Compare these numbers with your pay stubs to make sure everything matches.

Step 5: Add Up Your Income

Put all your W-2, 1099, and other income together to see how much money you made this year.

📝 Professor Tip: Check each form carefully — don’t forget small jobs or interest.

Step 6: Choose Deductions

You can pick the standard deduction (easy) or itemize (list big expenses like donations).

📝 Professor Tip: Use whichever lowers your tax the most.

Step 7: Apply Credits

Credits directly reduce your tax bill. Examples: Child tax credit, education credits.

📝 Professor Tip: Check every credit you qualify for — it could increase your refund!

Step 8: Calculate Your Tax

Subtract deductions and credits from your income to see how much tax you owe.

📌 Professor Tip: Use tax tables or software for accuracy.

Step 9: Apply Payments

Add up taxes already paid (from your W-2) and any estimated payments.

📝 Professor Tip: This helps us know if you get a refund or owe money.

Step 10: Determine Refund or Amount Owed

If your payments are more than your tax, you get a refund. If less, you owe money.

📌 Professor Tip: Double-check numbers before sending!

Step 11: Sign & Submit

Don’t forget to sign your return. Unsigned forms get rejected!

📝 Professor Tip: Keep a copy for your records.
