Step 1 — Gather Your Papers

  • W‑2 from your job
  • 1099 forms for freelance or interest
  • Receipts for deductions (education, charity)
  • Last year’s tax return (if you have it)

💬 Professor Hootington: “Think of this as collecting all your school supplies before starting a project. You can’t work without them!”

Step 2 — Fill in Your Personal Info

Enter your name, address, Social Security number, spouse info (if married), and dependents.

💬 Professor Hootington: “Check every letter and number. Your Social Security number is like your tax fingerprint!”

Step 3 — Add Up Your Income (Form 1040 Lines 1–8)

Line 1a – Wages, salaries, tips

All the money you earned from jobs (copy Box 1 from your W-2 forms).

💬 Professor Hootington: “Add all W-2s if you have multiple jobs. Double-check numbers!”

⬇️

Line 2a – Tax-exempt interest

Interest that doesn’t get taxed, like certain government accounts.

💬 Professor Hootington: “This may not be taxed, but include it so everything is accurate.”

⬇️

Line 2b – Taxable interest

Other bank or investment interest that can be taxed.

💬 Professor Hootington: “Add all interest from savings or CDs from 1099-INT forms.”

⬇️

Step 4 — Adjustments to Income (Schedule 1)

Line 9 – Adjustments

Subtract things like student loan interest, educator expenses, self-employment deductions, IRA contributions, and HSA contributions.

💬 Professor Hootington: “These lower your taxable income—more money in your pocket!”

Line 10 – Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

Total income minus adjustments. 💬 “This is your clean income number for the IRS.”

Step 5 — Deductions (Lines 11–12, Schedule A)

Line 11 – Standard or itemized deduction

Choose standard deduction (everyone gets this) or itemize using Schedule A.

Schedule A – Itemized Deductions

Medical, mortgage, state taxes, charitable donations.

💬 Professor Hootington: “Pick the bigger deduction to reduce your tax.”

Line 12 – Taxable income

AGI minus deductions. 💬 “This is the number the government will tax.”

Step 6 — Tax & Additional Taxes (Lines 13–15, Schedule 2)

Line 13 – Tax

Tax owed based on taxable income.

Schedule 2 – Additional Taxes

Self-employment tax, AMT, household employment taxes.

Line 15 – Total tax

Add regular and additional taxes. 💬 “Double-check these numbers!”

Step 7 — Payments & Credits (Lines 16–20, Schedule 3)

Line 16 – Federal tax withheld

From your W-2/1099 forms.

Line 17 – Estimated payments

Any tax payments you sent during the year.

Schedule 3 – Credits

Education, retirement, and other nonrefundable/refundable credits.

Line 20 – Total payments

Sum of payments and credits. 💬 “These reduce your tax owed and can increase your refund.”

Step 8 — Refund or Amount Owed (Lines 21–25)

Compare total tax vs total payments.

If payments > tax → refund. If tax > payments → you owe money.

Line 25 – Sign & Date

💬 Professor Hootington: “Sign your return or it’s like turning in homework unsigned!”

Step 9 — Supporting Schedules & Forms

💬 Professor Hootington: “These feed numbers into your 1040. Finish them first if you need them.”

  • Schedule A – Itemized Deductions
  • Schedule B – Interest & Dividends
  • Schedule C – Business/Self-Employment Income
  • Schedule D – Capital Gains & Losses
  • Schedule E – Rental & Partnership Income
  • Schedule F – Farm Income
  • Schedule SE – Self-Employment Tax
  • Schedule 8812 – Child Tax Credit
  • Form 8863 – Education Credits
  • Form 8880 – Retirement Savings Contributions Credit

💬 Professor Hootington Tip: “Take it one schedule at a time, and always double-check the numbers before transferring to 1040.”
