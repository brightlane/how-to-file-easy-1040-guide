🦉 Professor Hootington’s Tax Filing Guide

✅ PART 1 — GATHERING YOUR DOCUMENTS

📄 Step 1: Collect your income forms

  • W-2s from your employer(s)
  • 1099 forms for freelance or interest/dividend income
  • Other income records (rental, side hustles, tips)
Tip: Organize everything by type. Professor Hootington says: “A tidy desk is a tidy tax return!”

📄 Step 2: Collect your expense & deduction records

  • Receipts for charitable donations
  • Medical expenses (if itemizing)
  • Education expenses (tuition, books)
Tip: Keep digital copies to avoid losing receipts.

📄 Step 3: Gather personal info & ID numbers

  • Social Security numbers (yours, spouse’s, dependents)
  • Bank account info for direct deposit
Tip: Double-check all numbers—one wrong digit can delay your refund!

✅ PART 2 — STEP-BY-STEP FILING & ADVANCED TIPS

🦉 How to add up your income (Lines 4–8)

  • Line 4 – Interest you earned from banks
  • Line 5 – Dividends from stocks or investments
  • Line 6 – IRA distributions or pensions
  • Line 7 – Social Security benefits
  • Line 8 – Other income (gambling, freelance, tips)
Extra Tips:
  • Check each form carefully (1099-INT, 1099-DIV, etc.)
  • Don’t forget side hustles or small jobs
  • Add everything accurately to avoid errors
  • Professor Hootington says: “Think of it like adding all the pieces of a puzzle—you need each piece!”

🦉 How to subtract deductions

  • Standard deduction or itemized deductions
  • Education expenses
  • Charitable donations
Extra Tips: Deductions lower your taxable income. Use the larger of standard or itemized deductions.

🦉 How to apply tax credits

  • Child tax credit
  • Earned income credit
  • Education credits (e.g., American Opportunity Credit)
Extra Tips: Credits directly reduce your tax bill. Professor Hootington reminds: “Credits = money back in your pocket!”
